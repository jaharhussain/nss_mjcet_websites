ABOUT US

“To serve is beautiful, but only if it is done with joy and a whole hear and a free mind.”

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is an Indian government-sponsored public service program conducted by the Department of Youth Affairs and Sports of the Government of India. Popularly known as NSS, the scheme was launched in Gandhiji’s Centenary year, 1969. It is aimed at developing students’ personality through community service. NSS is a voluntary association of young people in Colleges, Universities and at +2 level working for a campus-community linkage.

Our Motto

• NOT ME BUT YOU

Objectives

Understanding the community in which they work Understanding themselves in relation to the community Recognizing the needs and problems of the community and including them in the problem-solving process Fostering a sense of social and civil responsibility with each other Knowledge to find practical solutions to problems for individuals and communities that utilize them Acquisition of leadership qualities and democratic attitudes

Scope

It aids in the development of a positive attitude, self-confidence, courage, and patience. It aids in the development of his or her ability to perform specific tasks, the qualities of an organiser and administrator, and the overall development of personality. He or she has the opportunity to observe the community closely and thus gain an understanding of human nature in relation to his or her surroundings. Has the opportunity to meet people from various walks of life.

Code of Conduct

• Collaborate with other clubs that have similar goals to the Association and are organized to support and promote activities such as the Association.

• Club members should work as a team and the club will enhance its value

• Commitment to quality service and performance.

• Promote club profits.

• It will be a positive role model.

• Be fair, compassionate, and honest with others.

• Being an expert in all actions.
